ACTA - Not In My Name

When industry lobbies claim to advocate in favour of ACTA on behalf of artists, some of them stand up to make their voice heard.

ACTA: Not in Our Name

ACTA (Anti-Counterfeiting Trade Agreement) is a treaty negotiated in secret by a few countries, circumventing democratic arenas and international organizations. It aims at setting the ground for new measures to prevent Internet users from sharing files with one another. Critics judge that these measures endanger the freedom of Internet communication and put pressure on private companies to monitor, censor and sanction, which should be done by the police and judges. As was too often the case, some industry or institutional interest groups supporting these measures will pretend that they speak in the name of authors, artists and other creative players. We simply tell them: Not in Our Name. The public is not our enemy. We need an equitable remuneration of all contributors to creative activities and a good environment for the production of new works, but not at the expense of endangering the free Internet, a precious good for us, as for all other
